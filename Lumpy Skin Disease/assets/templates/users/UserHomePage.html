Understanding Lumpy Skin Disease (LSD)

Cattle are susceptible to a viral infection called Lumpy Skin Disease (LSD), which was first discovered in Africa and is currently spreading to the Middle East, Asia, and some regions of Eastern Europe. Symptoms of the condition include fever, drooling, frequent tearing, and prominent skin nodules.

The Neethling virus, which causes LSD, leads to irreversible skin damage in affected cattle. This condition can also result in infertility, stunted growth, miscarriages, a decrease in milk production, and, in extreme circumstances, death.

Diagnosis and Importance

Diagnosis of LSD can be confirmed through PCR testing, virus isolation, and histopathology analysis. Given the variety of diseases that can harm animals, prompt and accurate diagnosis is essential to mitigate the severe consequences of LSD.
