About the Disease

Understanding Lumpy Skin Disease (LSD)

Lumpy Skin Disease is a viral infection affecting cattle, first discovered in Africa and now spreading across the Middle East, Asia, and parts of Eastern Europe. Caused by the Neethling virus, it presents as fever, excessive drooling, frequent tearing, and prominent nodular skin lesions.

The disease leads to irreversible skin damage and can cause infertility, stunted growth, miscarriages, a drop in milk production, and in severe cases, death — making early detection critical for farmers.
