About the Disease

Understanding Lumpy Skin Disease (LSD)

Lumpy Skin Disease is a viral infection affecting cattle, first discovered in Africa and now spreading across the Middle East, Asia, and parts of Eastern Europe. It is caused by the Neethling virus, a member of the Capripoxvirus genus, and manifests as fever, excessive drooling, nasal discharge, and prominent nodular lesions across the skin.

Beyond visible lesions, LSD inflicts serious economic consequences — infertility, stunted growth, miscarriages, a sharp drop in milk production, and in severe cases, death. The irreversible skin damage reduces hide quality, further compounding livestock losses for farmers.

Diagnosis Methods

Confirming LSD — Clinical Steps

Visual Symptom Assessment

Identification of skin nodules, fever, and mucous membrane discharge during field examination.

PCR Testing

Polymerase chain reaction testing on tissue or blood samples to confirm viral presence.

Virus Isolation & Histopathology

Laboratory analysis of cell cultures and tissue samples to characterize the infection stage and severity.
